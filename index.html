Meta Ads Lead Generation Tier Targeting
Meta Lead Gen: 1,098+ Leads at ₹49–50/lead using Tier-Based Targeting
Structured Meta lead generation campaign using smart tier audience segmentation. Delivered 1,098+ leads with cost per lead as low as ₹49–₹50 on ₹1,55,932 spend across 8,81,024 impressions.
1,098+Total leads generated
₹49–50Cost per lead
₹1,55,932Total spend
❓ What was the core challenge?
The client needed a high volume of quality leads at a controlled cost. Most advertisers struggle at ₹120–₹300 per lead with inconsistent quality. The goal was to bring CPL below ₹60 while maintaining lead quality through smart audience structure.
🎯 What strategy was used?
Tier-based audience segmentation — Tier 1, Tier 400, and Tier 500 campaigns. Each tier had its own budget cap, creative, and optimization logic. Winning ad sets were scaled; underperformers were paused immediately. Continuous CPL-based optimization kept costs in check.

Campaign Breakdown

  • Total Spend: ₹1,55,932.80
  • Total Impressions: 8,81,024
  • Total Reach: 2,83,024+
  • Leads: 1,098+ (Tier-1-500 campaign)
  • CPL: ₹49–₹50 per lead

What Worked

  • Tier-based audience segmentation
  • Daily budget caps per tier
  • CPL-based continuous optimization
  • Scaling winners, killing losers fast
  • Structured testing before scaling

Meta Ads E-commerce 5.81x ROAS
Meta E-commerce: ₹28K spend → ₹1.52L+ revenue — 5.81x ROAS, 100 purchases
Turned ₹28,262 in ad spend into ₹1,52,000+ in tracked revenue in just over a month. One campaign alone delivered ₹1,39,344 in purchase value at 5.81x ROAS and ₹272 cost per purchase.
5.81xROAS achieved

❓ What was the problem?
The client was running ads but getting inconsistent ROAS — sometimes 2x, sometimes nothing. They were spreading budget evenly across campaigns without tracking which ones actually drove purchases. Cost per purchase was rising with no clear strategy to fix it.
🎯 What made this campaign different?
We focused 100% on ROAS, not vanity metrics. Deep audience research, data-backed creative testing, and daily cost per acquisition tracking. Budget was only scaled when numbers justified it. Underperformers were killed fast. Winners were scaled confidently.

Strategy Breakdown

  • Deep audience research before launch
  • Data-backed creative testing
  • Smart budget allocation by performance
  • Daily monitoring of cost per acquisition
  • Aligned ads with real buying psychology

Meta Ads Real Estate Lead Generation
Real Estate Meta Campaign: 4,623+ Leads at ₹54/lead — ₹2.5L spend
Large-scale real estate lead generation campaign. Generated 4,623+ qualified leads at ₹54 average CPL on ₹2,50,000+ spend. Consistent increase in qualified site visits — far below industry average CPL of ₹120–₹300.
4,623+Leads generated
₹54Avg cost per lead
₹2,50,000+Total ad spend
❓ Why do most real estate campaigns struggle?
Most developers try to fix poor results by increasing budget — but the real problem is structure. When targeting doesn't match buyer intent, and creatives don't build trust and aspiration together, the algorithm has to "guess" your audience. That's what drives costs up to ₹120–₹300 per lead.
🎯 What drove ₹54 CPL consistently?
Three things: (1) Targeting matched real buyer intent — not just demographics. (2) Creatives built both aspiration and trust simultaneously. (3) The follow-up system was designed to push leads toward site visits, not just form fills. Better inputs = better leads = more site visits = better sales.

Campaign Numbers

  • Total Spend: ₹2,50,000+
  • Leads Generated: 4,623+
  • Avg CPL: ₹54
  • Industry avg CPL: ₹120–₹300
  • Result: 60–80% below market CPL

Key Success Factors

  • Intent-based audience targeting
  • Aspirational + trust-building creatives
  • Site visit-focused follow-up system
  • No complicated funnels
  • Algorithm-friendly campaign structure
